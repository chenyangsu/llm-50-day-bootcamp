Day 41 — LoRA: fine-tuning without the optimizer state

Wednesday, 2 September 2026 · Week 6 · Scaling and post-training · Study day · 3-4 h

← Day 40 · All 50 days · Day 42 →

On Day 34 you updated every weight in the model. Today you update a fraction of a percent of them and measure what that costs in task metrics and what it buys in memory. The paper’s claim is that the update you need is low-rank; your job is to check whether it holds on your task.

What you should be able to do tonight

  • Predict, before you run anything, the ratio of peak GPU memory between the Day 34 full fine-tune and a LoRA run, and justify the prediction in terms of Adam’s optimizer state rather than the weights themselves.
  • State what r and alpha each control, and why the update is scaled by alpha/r.
  • Report trainable parameter count, peak memory, wall-clock and your Day 35 metrics at r = 2, 8 and 32 against the Day 34 baseline.

Tasks 190 min

55 min Read LoRA Section 1, Section 4 (the W0 + BA parametrization, B initialized to zero and A Gaussian, the alpha/r scaling, and which Transformer matrices they adapt), and Section 7 - 7.1 on which matrices matter, 7.2 on how small r can get away with being, 7.3 on what the update amplifies. Skip the GLUE tables in Section 5.

15 min Write your predictions down before touching the GPU: peak memory ratio, wall-clock ratio, and the accuracy delta on your held-out set. Date them. You will be wrong about at least one and the point is to know which.

80 min Wrap the Day 34 training script with PEFT - LoraConfig(r=8, lora_alpha=16, target_modules=[...]), get_peft_model, then print_trainable_parameters() - keeping the same base model, same split and same seed as Day 34. Run r = 2, 8 and 32 with lora_alpha fixed at 16, recording torch.cuda.max_memory_allocated() and wall-clock for each.

40 min Fill in a four-row table - Day 34 full fine-tune, r = 2, r = 8, r = 32 - with trainable parameters, trainable fraction, peak memory, wall-clock, saved checkpoint size on disk, and your Day 35 metrics. Write three sentences naming which prediction was wrong and why.
